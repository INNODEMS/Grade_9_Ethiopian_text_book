    Section 1.2 Linear Equations

    Activity 1.2.1.

    Solve the following simultanios equation using the elimination method:
    \begin{align*} 4x-2y=\amp 7\\ 2x+y=\amp 7 \end{align*}
    Hint.
    • Multiply each equation by a suitable number to make the multiplying factor to one unknown equal in both equation.
    • Eliminate one unknown by either subtracting or adding the two equations formed
    • Solve for the remaining unknown.
    • Use the soluition to solve the other unknown.
    • The ubove is the elimination method of solving simultaneous equation
    Solution.
    \begin{align*} 3x-2y=\amp 7.........(i)\\ 2x+y=\amp 7 .........(ii) \end{align*}
    Multiply equation (ii) by 2 to get:
    \(4x+2y=14........(iii)\)
    \begin{align*} 3x-2y=\amp 7.......(i)\\ +4x+2y=\amp14........(iii) \text{add(i) to (iii)} \\ 7x=\amp 21\\ x=\amp 3 \end{align*}
    substitute \(x=3\) in \(3x-2y=7\)
    \begin{align*} 3(3)-2y= \amp 7\\ 9-2y=\amp 7\\ 9-7=\amp 2y\\ 2=\amp 2y \\ 1=\amp y \\ \text{Therefore},x=\amp3 \amp and \amp y=\amp1 \end{align*}
    \begin{equation} x=3 \text{and} y=1\tag{1.2.1} \end{equation}
    Our soluition is in (1.2.1)
