    <title>conversion of units of length</title>
    
  
  <activity>
    <statement>
        <p>
        Converting units of length.
      </p>
      <sidebyside>
      <p>
        Washika wants to go up the stairs. The staircases are labelled centimetres <m>\text{(cm) }</m>, decimetres <m>\text{ (dm) }</m>,
        metres <m>\text{ (m) }</m>, Decametres <m>\text{ (Dm) }</m> and Hectometres <m>\text{ (Hm) }</m>
         Every step he makes is equivalent to ten times the previous step .
         from the picture above,
        
      </p>
      <ol>
        <li>How many decimetres are there in a metr</li>
        <li>How many decimetres are there in a Decametre?</li>
        <li>How many metrea are there in a Hectometre?</li>
      </ol>

      <image source="/staircase.png" width="100%">
        <shortdescription>length conversion</shortdescription>
      </image>
      </sidebyside>
      
        <p>
            Conversion of length.
        </p>
        <p>
            To perfom the operations involving length, measurements must be of the same units.
        </p>
        <p>
            <ol>
                <li>
                    <p>
                        On a ruler, count the number of <m>\text{ millimetres }</m> in <m>1\text{ cm }</m>.
                    </p>
                </li>

                <li>
                    <p>
                        Measure and cut  a string <m>10\text{ cm }</m> long.
                    </p>
                    <p>
                        <me> Note: 10\text{ cm } =1\text{ decimetre(dm) } </me>
                    </p>
                </li>
            
                <li>
                    <p>
                        Find the number of times it fits on a string of length <m>1 \text{ metre } </m>
                    </p>
                </li>
            
                <li>
                    <p>
                        Fill in the table below.
                    </p>
                </li>
            </ol>
        </p>
        <table>
          <title />
          <tabular halign="center" top="medium" left="medium" bottom="medium" right="medium">
            <row> 
              <cell> ______mm = 1cm</cell>

        <table>
          <title>Learning point.</title>
          <tabular halign="center" top="medium" left="medium" bottom="medium" right="medium">
            <row>
              <cell>10mm  = 1cm</cell>
            </row>
            <row>
              <cell>10cm  = 1dm</cell>
            </row>
            <row>
              <cell>10dm  = 1m</cell>
            </row>
            <row>
              <cell>10m  = 1Dm</cell>
            </row>
            <row>
              <cell>10Dm = 1Hm</cell>
            </row>
            <row>
              <cell>10Hm  = 1km</cell>
            </row>
          </tabular>
        </table>

       <p>
        The standard unit for measuring length internationally is <m>\text{ Metres }.</m>
        </p>
        <p>
          a. We multiply a unit by 10 to convert it to the nexr in descending order.
          <me>\text{ Hm } \xrightarrow[] {\times 10} \text{ Dm } \xrightarrow[]{\times 10} \text{ m } \xrightarrow[]{\times 10} \text{ dm } \xrightarrow[]{\times 10}\text{ cm }</me>
        </p>
        <p>
          b. We divide a unit by 10 to convert it to the nexr in descending order.
          <me>\text{ Hm } \xrightarrow[]{\div10} \text{ Dm } \xrightarrow[]{\div 10}\text{ m } \xrightarrow[]{\div 10}\text{ dm } \xrightarrow[]{\div 10}\text{ cm }</me>
        </p>
    </statement>
</activity>
<example>
<statement>
    <p>
       Convert <m>40\text{ m } </m> to <m>\text{ Dm } </m>
        </p>
</statement>
<solution>
    <p>
        <m>10 \text{ m } =1\text{ Dm }</m>
    </p>
    <p>
        <m> 40 \text{ m } = {\frac{40}{10}} \text{ Dm }</m>
    </p>
    <p>
        <m>=4 \text{ Dm }</m>
    </p>
</solution>
</example>
<example>
    <statement>
        <p>
            
            Convert <m>1500 \text{ m }</m> to <m>\text{ Hm }</m> 
        </p>
    </statement>
    <solution>
        <p>
            <m>100 \text{ m } = 1 \text{ Hm }</m>
        </p>
        <p>
            <m>1500 \text{ m }= {\frac{1500}{100} \text{ Hm }}</m>
        </p>
        <p>
            <m>= 15 \text{ Hm }</m>
        </p>
    </solution>
</example>
<example>
    <statement>
        <p>
            Convert <m>100\text{ Hm }</m> to <m>\text{ dm }</m>
        </p>
       
    </statement>
    <solution>
        <p>
            <m> 1\text{ Hm } = 1000 \text{ dm }</m>
        </p>
        <p>
            <m> 100\text{ Hm } = (100 \times 1000) \text{ dm }</m>
        </p>
        <p>
            <m>100,000 \text{ dm }</m>
        </p>
    </solution>
</example>
