    <subsubsection xml:id="subsubsec-Hectares">
      <title>Hectares</title>
      <p>
        A hectare is a unit of measuring large areas.
      </p>
      <p>
        One hectare is equal to <m>10000 m^2</m>
      </p>
      <p>
        Therefore,
      </p>
      <p>
        <m>1 \text{hectare}(ha) = 10000m^2</m>
      </p>
      <p>
        <m>1 \text{hectare}(ha) = 2.47 \text{acre}</m>
      </p>
      <example>
      <statement>
        <p>
          Convert <m>25 \text{ha}</m> into <m>\text{ares}</m>
        </p>
      </statement>
      <hint>
        <p>
          Use <m>1 hectare(ha) = 2.47 acre</m>
        </p>
      </hint>
      <solution>
        <p>
          <md>
            <mrow>  1ha=\amp 2.47 \text{acre} </mrow>
            <mrow> 25 ha=\amp (2.47 \times 25) \text{acres}</mrow>
            <mrow> =\amp 61.75 acres</mrow>
          </md>
        </p>
      </solution>
      </example>
      <example>
        <statement>

            find the area of the figure below in hectares.

  <subsection xml:id="subsec-Area-of-a-rectangle">
    <title>Area of a rectangle</title>
    <p>
      Area of a rectangle <m>=</m> length <m>\times</m> width
    </p>
    <p>
      length <m>=</m> Area <m>\div</m> width
    </p>
    <p>
      width <m>=</m> Area <m>\div</m> length
    </p>
    <example>

        <p>
          Find the area of a rectangle whose length is <m>12 cm</m> 
          and width is <m>8cm</m>.
        </p>

            <mrow> =\amp 96 cm^2 </mrow>
          </md>
        </p>
      </solution>
    </example>

  <subsection xml:id="subsec-Area-of-a-rhombus">
    <title>Area of a rhombus</title>
    <p>
      A rhombus is a type of quadrilateral that is a special kind of parallelogram. The key characteristics of a rhombus are:
    </p>
    <p>
      <ol>
        <li>
          <p>
            Opposite sides are parallel.
          </p>
        </li>
        <li>
          <p>
            All sides have equal length.
          </p>
        </li>
        <li>
          <p>
            Opposite angles are equal, and adjacent angles are supplementary (they add up to 180Â°).
          </p>
        </li>
        <li>
          <p>
            The diagonals of a rhombus bisect each other at right angles (90Â°), meaning they intersect at 
            a right angle and divide each other into two equal parts.
          </p>
        </li>
      </ol>
    </p>
    <p>
      The formula for the area <m>A</m> of a rhombus can be given in two common ways:
    </p>
    <p>
      <ul>
        <li>
          <p>
            Using the diagonals: <me>A=\frac{1}{2}\times d_1 \times d_2</me>
          </p>
          <p>
            where:
          </p>
          <p>
            <m>d_1 </m>and <m>d_2</m> are the lengths of the two diagonals.
          </p>
        </li>
        <li>
          <p>
            Using the base and height: <me>A= \text{base} \times \text{height}</me>
          </p>
          <p>
            Where the base is the length of any side (since all sides are equal) and the 
            height is the perpendicular distance between opposite sides.
          </p>
        </li>
      </ul>
    </p>
    <activity>
      <statement>
        <p>
          Calculate the area of the figure given it's 
          diagonals are <m>6cm</m> and <m>8cm</m>.
        </p>
        <image source="/Rhombus.png" width="40%">
          <shortdescription>Rhombus</shortdescription>
        </image>
      </statement>
      <solution>
        <p>
          Area of a rhombus <m>= \frac{1}{2}</m> short diagonals <m>\times</m> long diagonal.
        </p>
        <p>
          <md>
            <mrow> A=\amp \frac{1}{2}d_1 d_2</mrow>
            <mrow> A=\amp \frac{1}{2} (6 \times 8)cm^2 </mrow>
            <mrow> A=\amp 24 cm^2 </mrow>
          </md>
        </p>
      </solution>
    </activity>

    </p>
    <p>

          <p>
            The area of a trapezium <m>= \frac{1}{2} (a+b)h</m>
          </p>
          <p>
            where: <m>(a+b)</m> is the sum of the parallel sides and <m>h</m> is the perpendicular 
            height of the trapezium.
          </p>
        </li>
      </ul>
    </p>
    <activity>
      <statement>
        <p>

          What is the area of the plot of land?
        </p>

          <md>
            <mrow> \text{Area of trapezium}=\amp \frac{1}{2} (a+b)h</mrow>

            <mrow> =\amp \frac{1}{2} \times 360m \times 50m </mrow>
            <mrow> =\amp 9000m^2</mrow>
          </md>
        </p>
      </solution>
    </activity>

          </p>

  <subsection xml:id="subsec-Area-of-a-semicircle">
    <title>Area of a semicircle</title>
    <p>
      A semicircle is a circle divided into two equal parts.
    </p>
    <p>
      Since we now know the area of a circle, the area of 
      a semicircle is the area of a circle divided by two as shown bellow;
    </p>
    <p>
      <m>\text{Area of semicircle}=\frac{1}{2} \times \pi r^2</m>.
    </p>
    <activity>
      <statement>
        <p>
          Find the area of the semicircle given below.
        </p>
        <image width="40%">
        <latex-image>
          \begin{tikzpicture}
          \draw[very thick,fill=white](0,0)  arc[start angle=0,end angle=180,radius=5cm];
          \draw [thick,fill=black](0,0) --(-10,0);
          \node at (-5,0.5){$28cm$};
          
          \end{tikzpicture}
        </latex-image>
          
        </image>
      </statement>
      <solution>
        <p>
          <md>
            <mrow> \text{radius} =\amp \frac{\text{diameter}}{2} </mrow>
            <mrow> r=\amp \frac{28}{2}cm </mrow>
            <mrow> r=\amp 14cm </mrow>
          </md>
        </p>
        <p>
          <md>
            <mrow> \text{Area of semicircle}=\amp \frac{1}{2} \pi \times r^2 </mrow>
            <mrow> =\amp \frac{1}{2} \pi \times r \times r</mrow>
            <mrow> =\amp\frac{1}{2}\times\frac{22}{7} \times 14^2cm^2 </mrow>
            <mrow> =\amp \frac{1}{2} \times\frac{22}{7} \times 196 cm^2</mrow>
            <mrow> =\amp\frac{1}{2} \times 616cm^2 </mrow>
            <mrow> =\amp 308cm^2</mrow>
          </md>
          
        </p>
      </solution>
    </activity>
    
  </subsection>

    <title>Area of borders and combined shapes</title>
    <p>

      its requred that you are in a position to calculate areas of any figure given.

    <p>
      <ul>
        <li>

            Combine figures can either be circle inside a circle, 
            rectangle inside a rectangle, circle inside a trapezium and many more.

        </li>
        <li>
          <p>
            Its requred to calculate areas acording to the instractions given in 
            an equation.
          </p>
        </li>
      </ul>
    </p>
    <p>
      Some worked examples are as below.
    </p>
    <activity>
      <statement>
        <p>
          Work out the area of the figure bellow
        </p>
        <image width="50%">
        <latex-image>
          \begin{tikzpicture}
            \draw[very thick,fill=white] (0,0) --(5,0) --(8,0) --(5,3) --(0,3);
            \draw [very thick,fill=white]   (0,3) arc[start angle=90,end angle=270,radius=1.5cm] ;
            \draw [dashed,fill=black] (0,0) --(0,3);
            \draw [dashed,fill=black] (5,0) --(5,3);
            \draw [very thick, &lt;-&gt;] (0,-0.5) --(5,-0.5);
            \draw [very thick, &lt;-&gt;] (5,-0.5) --(8,-0.5) ;
            \node at (2.5,-1){$20cm$};
            \node at (6,-1){$18cm$};
            \node at (0.5,1.5){$14cm$};
          \end{tikzpicture}
        </latex-image>
          
        </image>
      </statement>
      <solution>
       <p>
          <md>
            <mrow> \text{Area of the rectangle}=\amp l \times w </mrow>
            <mrow> =\amp 20cm \times 14cm </mrow>
            <mrow> =\amp280 cm^2 </mrow>
          </md>
       </p>
       <p>
          <md>
            <mrow> \text{Area of the triangle}=\amp \frac{1}{2} bh </mrow>
            <mrow> =\amp  \frac{1}{2} \times 18cm \times 14cm  </mrow>
            <mrow>= \amp 126cm^2</mrow>
          </md>
        </p>
       <p>
          <md>
            <mrow> \text{Area of the semicircle}=\amp \frac{1}{2} \pi r^2 </mrow>
            <mrow> =\amp \frac{1}{2} \frac{22}{7} \times 7cm \times 7cm</mrow>
            <mrow> =\amp77cm^2 </mrow>
          </md>
       </p>
       <p>
          <md>
            <mrow> \text{Area of the combined shapes}=\amp \text{area of all the three figures calculated} </mrow>
            <mrow> =\amp 280cm^2 +126cm^2+77cm^2 </mrow>
            <mrow> =\amp 483cm^2</mrow>
          </md>
       </p>
       <p>
         <m>Alternatively:</m> add up the area of the trapezium and the semicircle.
       </p>

          <md>
            <mrow> \text{Area of the trapezium}=\amp \frac{1}{2} h(a+b)  </mrow>
              <mrow> =\amp  \frac{1}{2} \times 14(20+18+20) </mrow>
              <mrow> = \amp 7cm \times 58cm </mrow>
              <mrow> = \amp406cm^2 </mrow>
          </md>
       </p>
       <p>
         Area of the semi circle <m>=77cm^2</m>
       </p>
       <p>
          <md>
            <mrow>\text{Area of the combined shape} =\amp \text{Area of the two figure} </mrow>
            <mrow> = \amp 406cm^2+77cm^2 </mrow>
            <mrow> =\amp 483cm^2 </mrow>
          </md>
       </p>

        
      </solution>
