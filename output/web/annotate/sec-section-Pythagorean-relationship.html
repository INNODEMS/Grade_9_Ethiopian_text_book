                A right-angle triangle has three sides:

              </p>

                the <em>hypotenuse</em>.

  <p>
    The pythagorean relationship.
  </p>
  <p>
    <ul>
      <li>
        <p>
          It state that, the sum of the squares of the 
          two shorter sides is equal to the squre of the 
          longer side (hypotenuse).
        </p>
       </li>
    </ul>
  </p>
  <sidebyside>
    <image source="/Sides-triangle.png" width="40">
      <shortdescription>Sides</shortdescription>
    </image>
    <p>
      <ul>
        <li>
          <p>
            From the fugure alongside, side a is the <m>base</m>,
            side b is the <m>height</m> and side c is the 
            <m>hypotenuse</m>.Therfore the relationship is,
          </p>
        </li>
        <li>
          <p>
            <me>
              a^2+b^2=c^2
            </me>
          </p>
        </li>
        <li>
          <p>
            <md>
              <mrow>b^2= \amp c^2-a^2</mrow>
              <mrow>a^2= \amp c^2-b^2</mrow>
            </md>
          </p>
        </li>
      </ul>
    </p>

  </sidebyside>
  <example>
    <statement>
      <p>
        Find the length of the side marked y in 
        the right-angled triangle below.
      </p>
      <image source="/Question.png" width="40%">
        <shortdescription>question</shortdescription>
      </image>
    </statement>
    <solution>
      <p>
        <md>
          <mrow> c^2=\amp a^2+b^2 </mrow>
          <mrow> y^2=\amp (8 cm)^2+ (6 cm)^2 </mrow>
          <mrow> y^2=\amp(8 cm \times 8 cm)+ (6cm \times 6 cm) </mrow>
          <mrow> y^2=\amp 64 cm^2 +36 cm^2 </mrow>
          <mrow> y^2=\amp 100 cm^2 </mrow>
          <mrow> y=\amp \sqrt{100 cm^2} </mrow>
          <mrow> y=\amp 10 cm </mrow>
        </md>
      </p>
      <p>
        Therefore,
      </p>
      <p>
        side <m>y</m> is <m>10 cm</m>
      </p>
    </solution>
  </example>

  <p>
    Application for pythagorean relationship
  </p>
  <example>
    <statement>
      <p>
        The base of a ladder is <m>5 m</m> from the base of a vertical 
        wall. A painter places the ladder such that it touches the top of the 
        wall at a point <m>12 m</m> above the ground.calculate the length 
        of the ladder (c).
      </p>
    </statement>
    <hint>
      <p>
        <ul>
          <li>
            <p>
              It is appropriate for the learner to draw the figure as discribed in the 
              question above.
            </p>
          </li>
          <li>
            <p>
              Use the pythagorean relationship that is; <m>a^2+b^2=c^2</m>
            </p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </p>
    </hint>
    <solution>
      <p>
        Using the pythagorean relationship that outlines that, <m>a^2+b^2=c^2</m> therefore,
      </p>
      <p>
        <md>
          <mrow> c^2=\amp(5^2+12^2)m^2 </mrow>
          <mrow> c^2= \amp(25+144)m^2 </mrow>
          <mrow> c^2=\amp 169 m^2 </mrow>
          <mrow> c=\amp \sqrt{169 m^2}</mrow>
          <mrow> c=\amp 13 m</mrow>
        </md>
      </p>
      <p>
        Therefore,the length of the ladder (c) is <m>13 m</m>
      </p>
    </solution>
  </example>
