  <title>Pythagorean relationship</title>
 <p>
  <ul>
    <li>
      <p>
        A triangle that contains a right angle 
        (an angle that measures <m>90^\circ</m>,symbolized by a small square "â–¡") is
        called a right triangle.
      </p>
    </li>
    <li>
      <p>
        The longest side of the right triangle (the side opposite the <m>90^\circ</m> angle)
        is called the hypotenuse and the other two (shorter) sides 
        are called the legs of the triangle.

      </p>
    </li>
    <li>
      <p>
        The legs of a right triangle are commonly labeled
        "<m>a</m>" and "<m>b</m>"while the hypotenuse 
        is labeled "<m>c.</m>
      </p>
    </li>
  </ul>
 </p>

  <p>
    The pythagorean relationship.
  </p>
  <p>
    <ul>
      <li>
        <p>
          It state that, the sum of the squares of the 
          two shorter sides is equal to the squre of the 
          longer side (hypotenuse).
        </p>
       </li>
    </ul>
  </p>
  <sidebyside>
    <image source="/sides.png" width="40">
      <shortdescription>Sides</shortdescription>
    </image>
    <p>
      <ul>
        <li>
          <p>
            From the fugure alongside, side a is the <m>base</m>,
            side b is the <m>height</m> and side c is the 
            <m>hypotenuse</m>.Therfore the relationship is, <m>a^2+b^2=c^2</m>.
          </p>
          <p>
            The relationship can be changed depending on the question asked  on the length to calculate as shown below.
          </p>
        </li>
        <li>
          <p>
            <me>
              c^2=a^2+b^2
            </me>
          </p>
          <p>
            <md>
              <mrow>b^2= \amp c^2-a^2</mrow>
              <mrow>a^2= \amp c^2-b^2</mrow>
            </md>
          </p>
        </li>
     
      </ul>
    </p>

  </sidebyside>
  <example>
    <statement>
      <p>
        Find the length of the side marked y in 
        the right-angled triangle below.
      </p>
      <image source="/Question.png" width="40%">
        <shortdescription>question</shortdescription>
      </image>
    </statement>
    
    <solution>
      <p>
        To find the length of any side of the right-angled triangle, we use the 
        pythagorean relationship which state that: <m>c^2=a^2+b^2 </m>.
      </p>
      <p>
        In the above example,
      </p>
      <p>
        <md>
          <mrow> a=\amp 6\,cm </mrow>
          <mrow> b=\amp 8\,cm </mrow>
          <mrow> c=\amp y </mrow>
        </md>
      </p>
      <p>
        Therefore,
      </p>
      <p>
        <md>
          <mrow> y^2=\amp (8 cm)^2+ (6 cm)^2 </mrow>
          <mrow> y^2=\amp(8 cm \times 8 cm)+ (6cm \times 6 cm) </mrow>
          <mrow> y^2=\amp 64 cm^2 +36 cm^2 </mrow>
          <mrow> y^2=\amp 100 cm^2 </mrow>
          <mrow> y=\amp \sqrt{100 cm^2} </mrow>
          <mrow> y=\amp 10 cm </mrow>
        </md>
      </p>
      <p>
        Therefore,
      </p>
      <p>
        side <m>y</m> is <m>10 cm</m>
      </p>
    </solution>
  </example>
