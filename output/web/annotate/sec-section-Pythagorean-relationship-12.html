<example>
  <statement>
    <p>
      The base of a ladder is <m>5 m</m> from the base of a vertical 
      wall. A painter places the ladder such that it touches the top of the 
      wall at a point <m>12 m</m> above the ground.calculate the length 
      of the ladder (c).
    </p>
  </statement>
  <hint>
    <p>
      <ul>
        <li>
          <p>
            It is appropriate for the learner to draw the figure as discribed in the 
            question above.
          </p>
        </li>
        <li>
          <p>
            Use the pythagorean relationship that is; <m>a^2+b^2=c^2</m>
          </p>
        </li>
      </ul>
    </p>
  </hint>
  <solution>
    <p>
      Using the pythagorean relationship that outlines that, <m>a^2+b^2=c^2</m> therefore,
    </p>
    <p>
      <md>
        <mrow> c^2=\amp(5^2+12^2)m^2 </mrow>
        <mrow> c^2= \amp(25+144)m^2 </mrow>
        <mrow> c^2=\amp 169 m^2 </mrow>
        <mrow> c=\amp \sqrt{169 m^2}</mrow>
        <mrow> c=\amp 13 m</mrow>
      </md>
    </p>
    <p>
      Therefore,the length of the ladder (c) is <m>13 m</m>
    </p>
  </solution>
</example>
