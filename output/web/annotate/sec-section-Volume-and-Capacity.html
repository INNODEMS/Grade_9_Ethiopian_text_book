  <subsection xml:id="subsec-cubic-metre-as-a-unit-of-measuing-volume">
    <title>Cubic metre as a unit of measuing volume</title>
    <activity>
     <sidebyside>
      <ul>
        <li>
          <p>
           what you need: Twelve sticks of length <m>1\text{m}</m>
          </p>
        </li>
        <li>
          <p>
            Old pieces of paper 
          </p>
        </li>
        <li>
          <p>
            A pair of scissors.
          </p>
        </li>
        <li>
          <p>
            A ruler
          </p>
        </li>
        <li>
          <p>
            Pieces of string.
          </p>
        </li>
          <li>
            <p>
              Join the twelve sticks to form a cube like the one shown on the right.
              use the pieces of paper to cover it to make a closed cube.
            </p>
          </li>
          <li>
            <p>
              what is the volume of the cube you have made?
            </p>
          </li>
          <li>
            <p>
              The cube you have made is called a cubic metre. It's sides are <m>1\text{m}</m> long.
            </p>
            <p>
              The volume of the cube is <m>1\text{m} /times 1\text{m} /times 1\text{m} = 1\text{m^3}</m>
            </p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      <image source="/cube.png">
        <shortdescription>making cubes</shortdescription>
      </image>
     </sidebyside>
     <p>
      Conversion of cubic metres to cubic centimetres.
     </p>
     <p>
      Measure the length, the width and the height of the cube in metres .
      Work out it's volume in cubic metres.
       <m>1\text{m} /times 1\text{m} /times 1\text{m} = 1\text{m^3}</m>
     </p>
     <p>
      Measure the length, the width and the height of the cube in centimetres.
      Work out it's volume in cubic centimetres.
       <m>100\text{cm} /times 100\text{cm} /times 100\text{cm} = 1,000,000\text{cm^3}</m>
     </p>
     <p>
      How many cubic centimetres is one cubic metre? <m>1,000,000\text{cm^3}</m>
      How can you convert the volume you got in cubic metres to cubic centimetres? 
      Since <m>1\text{m^3} = 1,000,000\text{cm^3}</m>
     </p>
     <p>
      Therefore to convert volume from cubic metres to cubic centimetres, we multiply the volume by 1,000,000.
     </p>
    </activity>
  </subsection>
